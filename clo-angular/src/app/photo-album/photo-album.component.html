Images of the Duke-Edinburgh Edition

This is a special album collecting all of the images from the entire run to date of the Duke-Edinburgh Edition of the Carlyle Letters, including all frontispieces and all internal images.

Album Two

captioned by TC / “ These things I mark, mournfully, as a kind of duty,—this evg Monday 7 Octr 1867—T.C. ” / this album with its laid-in addenda includes 52 images

Album Three

captioned by TC / “This seems to have been gathered mainly at Haddington (in perhaps 1859 &c): I know few of the figures; mournfully mark this I do (Monday night, 7 Octr 1869) T.C.” / “x” in TC’s hand indicates that he was unable to identify the figure) / this album includes 44 images

Album Four

almost certainly collected and arranged by JWC / many images annotated by TC / this album includes 105 images

Album Five

an assorted array, many pictures from TC and JWC’s era including material possibly removed from their portrait screens, other portraits and views collected, assembled, and arranged by Alexander Carlyle / this album contains 38 images

Album Six

“Personal to Alexander Carlyle, T.C.’s nephew. Probably done after T.C.’s death.” / the album generally consists of two slots per page; some slots are blank and thus marked, in square brackets / this album contains 84 images

Album Seven

“Personal to Alexander Carlyle, T.C.’s nephew. Probably done after T.C’s death.” / this album contains 140 images
